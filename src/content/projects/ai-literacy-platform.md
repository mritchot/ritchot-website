Workforce AI adoption has outrun workforce AI judgment. Most training on offer teaches tool familiarity and measures completion, and it skips what judgment actually rests on: the mechanics of how these models produce their output. Without that grounding, a learner has no basis for deciding what to hand to a model, how to specify the work, whether to trust what comes back, or who stays accountable for the result. I built this program to close that gap, and it grew directly out of my [capstone research](/docs/capstone.pdf) on teaching AI Literacy through the mechanics of how LLMs work. It consists of four modules (Context, Evidence, Mechanism, Application) that move a mid-career professional from the business case through the documented usage evidence into how the models actually generate text, and finally into applied practice, on a platform I built myself so the interaction design and data capture would not inherit an authoring tool's constraints. Start to finish, the build took roughly 210 hours of solo work. The full professional record — the needs analysis, the evaluation framework, and the build records — is published below.
